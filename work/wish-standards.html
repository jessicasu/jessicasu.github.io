Wish Standards

As part of a strategic initiative in Wish's turnaround strategy, I worked with the team to improve the quality and trust of the marketplace.

Context

Wish.com is notorious for its inexpensive products and long delivery times. These products are advertised at unbelievably low prices and often times customers receive their orders within weeks only to discover it’s not what they thought they ordered.

Memes poking fun at Wish's questionable product quality

These low product standards and merchant quality is largely due to Wish’s unclear expectations and lack of incentives to offer a good customer experience. As part of the turnaround strategy to improve the quality of merchants on our platform and to instill trust into our consumers, Wish introduced Wish Standards, a new tier based program that rewards top performing seller while disincentivizing poor performance.

Merchants are ranked on several criteria (to list some for example: average user rating, valid tracking rate, delivery rate) and placed on one of four tiers. Merchants with super and above standard tier standings are rewarded with higher pay out and product visibility while merchants on lower tiers are strongly encourage to improve their store performance before they are taken off the platform.

To ensure a seamless experience on the merchant and consumer sides, my design colleagues (Steven Park, Sherry Wu) and I took on different projects during fall 2021 to help launch this massive initiative. My priorities focused on creating a seller trust tier system via badging and designing infraction dispute process.

Badging

The Ask

I was tasked to create a badging system to communicate each of the performance tiers (Below standard, Standard, Above standard, Super). While designing, I had to take into mind:

  • On the merchant side, the badge should be used as an indication of their Wish Standards standing.

  • On the consumer side, the badges should be used as a way to convey quality and trust, giving consumers more confidence to purchase. Only “Above standard” and “Super” tier merchants would have a badge displayed with their products and storefront as a way to reward their store performance.

The Approach

Working closely with the consumer-side designer, we brainstormed when it would be most useful for a consumer to see the badge and on which pages the badge would live in the Wish app.

Considering how merchants and consumers would interact with the badges and using inspiration from other platforms, I created defining principles that make a good badge:

  1. It needs to stand out among the product listing and other badges. What can we use to make the badge stand out among the myriad of existing badges and product images? Use color, size, or animation.

  2. It needs to be clear. Consumers and merchants should be able to understand the logo. What can we do to make clear? Use universal symbols and colors or supporting text.

  3. It should be scalable. It can be used to represent different levels. What can we do? Be intentional about the color, shape, and stroke.

  4. It should be simple. The icon will be used on the product cards and should be distinguishable even if it’s in a small size.

Using these guiding principles, I designed badges to represent the following tiers: Bronze, Silver, Gold, and Platinum, using the colors and shape complexity to distinguish the different levels.

User Testing

During user testing, we discovered that while product listings with badges did convey quality and trust compared to listings without it, it wasn’t clear to the participants what was the difference between the Gold and Platinum badges.

With the help of the UX writer (Ericka Wang), we abstracted away the tier information on the consumer side and combined the Gold and Platinum tier merchants into “Premier merchants.” These Premier merchants would be denoted by a single badge in the Wish app to simplify the treatment we give to our good performing merchants.

The Result

Wish launched the new seller standards program on November 18, 2021 to its 500k merchants. Merchants were able to see their tier level and corresponding badge inside their Wish Standards performance dashboard. Wish also updated the customer experience so that Android users can begin identifying quality sellers and products via the badges.

Going from concepts to program launch in almost three months, we're excited to see Wish Standards come to life! Wish is focused on improving the quality and health of the Wish ecosystem and Wish Standards plays a crucial role to elevating the Wish experience for both merchants and consumers.
