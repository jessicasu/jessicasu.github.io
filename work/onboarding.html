Onboarding

I'm currently working with my team to improve our onboarding flow for suppliers. More updates to come!

Role: UX Designer

Teammates: Will (PM), Sarah (PMM), Abe (Eng Lead)

Status: In Progress

Background

RangeMe is an online marketplace that connects suppliers (brands like Coca Cola, Oreos, Chico Honey Co.) and buyers (retailers such as Target, Whole Foods Market, Safeway). The RangeMe platform lets supplier create online company profiles to showcase their products, which buyers can discover and reach out to.

Context

Suppliers must set up their profile and create a listing for their product to be searchable to retailers. When their profile is published, suppliers are more likely to receive a reach out from buyers and realize the value of RangeMe.

To complete onboarding, suppliers must complete the two pages of account creation and a lengthy form to make a product listing.

The Problem

Today we see massive drop-offs between each page of the sign up and product form. About 32% of total suppliers complete their first product.

Our Goal

Increase the percentage of suppliers who complete their first product

Early Research

To figure out which areas of the onboarding flow we should focus on, I used Heap and Fullstory to conduct early research, see how suppliers currently get through sign up, and identify problem areas.

Initial Hypotheses

  • Some users aren't suppliers. A significant portion of users who end up on RangeMe’s sign up page aren’t actually the correct users for RangeMe. Some suppliers seem to believe that RangeMe is a B2C platform, frequently answering our “Company name” field with inputs like “Don’t have.”

  • The perceived value of RangeMe isn’t enough motivation to add their first product. When suppliers land on the RangeMe Home page after creating an account, many wandered the site and was not sure what to do. Without a clear understanding of RangeMe, it would be very hard to a supplier to get through the all the steps.

Surveys and User Interviews

We sent a survey to over 300+ suppliers, asking "Why haven't you finished adding your product?" to validate our understanding of problems areas. Additionally, we interviewed 3 suppliers to gain a more detailed understanding of their struggles.

Key Findings

  • The value of RangeMe isn't clear. Several suppliers asked how RangeMe works and expressed skepticism in the platform. When a supplier is signing up, they face a lot of distractions, such as upgrade paths to RangeMe Premium, and question if a Free account is worth creating.

  • Some suppliers need help adding in their product. Our product form is over 40+ questions long and asks very niche questions. For less experienced suppliers, this form can be daunting and we currently do not offer much help to suppliers. Two of the three suppliers we spoke to mentioned wanting to speak with a real person for support.

Our Approach

Based on our findings, we believe that we can increase the percentage of suppliers who complete their first product by focusing in 4 specific areas: adding user motivation, clarifying RangeMe's value, reducing distractions, and offering more support. Because of our limited engineering capacity, we're focused on small but impactful changes.

Recent Improvements

To reduce distractions, we recently launched improvements to keep suppliers from being sidetracked.

Removing unnecessary screens in our welcome modal

Previously our welcome modal showed 3 slides. Two of the three slides were irrelevant given their stage of the funnel (the 1st slide gave a basic summary about RangeMe, the 3rd described a paid feature).

Previous modal

To help suppliers stay on track, we condensed the welcome modal to one screen that explains suppliers why they should add their product and links them to the product form.

New single frame modal

Changing the 1st step in our onboarding checklist to "Add product"

After completing the first two pages of sign up, suppliers are brought to their Home, where they see a checklist that helps them complete their profile. Previously, the first task they saw was "Confirm email." Out of all the tasks, "Add product" is most valuable for suppliers. By making it the 1st step to complete, it becomes more visible and provides additional context about why they should complete it.

More to Come! 😊

This project is still in progress. We're currently working on improvements to offer more motivation and support to suppliers. Check back for updates as more changes are launched.
