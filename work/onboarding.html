Onboarding

As part of a 2021 initiative, I'm working with my teammates to improve RangeMe's supplier onboarding flow.

Context

RangeMe is an online B2B marketplace that connects 200k+ suppliers and buyers in the consumer packaged goods (CPG) industry. As part of the platform, suppliers must set up their profile and create a product listing to be searchable to retailers. When their profiles are published, suppliers can receive reach outs from buyers and realize the value of RangeMe.

To create a profile, suppliers must complete two pages of account creation and a four paged product form.

Current supplier onboarding flow from sign up to the product form

The Problem

Today we see massive drop-offs between each page of the sign up and product form. About a third of total suppliers get to the product form and complete their first product.

Adding their product is crucial step that suppliers must take to start receiving reach outs from buyers. By not adding in their product, they are less likely to reach an "Ah-ha" moment and see the value RangeMe can provide them.

How might we increase the number of suppliers who complete their first product?

Surveys and User Interviews

I worked closely with our PM Will and PMM Sarah to understand why suppliers weren't completing their profiles. We sent a survey to over 300+ suppliers, asking "Why haven't you finished adding your product?" to validate our understanding of problems areas. Additionally, we interviewed 3 suppliers to gain a more detailed understanding of their struggles. We learned that:

  • The value of RangeMe isn't clear. Several suppliers asked how RangeMe works and expressed skepticism in the platform. When a supplier is signing up, they face a lot of distractions, such as upgrade paths to RangeMe Premium, and question if a Free account is worth creating.

  • Some suppliers need help adding in their product. Our product form is over 40+ questions long and asks very niche questions. For less experienced suppliers, this form can be daunting and we currently do not offer much help to suppliers. Two of the three suppliers we spoke to mentioned wanting to speak with a real person for support.

Our Approach

Based on our findings, we believe that we can increase the percentage of suppliers who complete their first product. Because of our limited engineering capacity, we're focused on small but impactful changes in 4 areas:

Reducing distractions

Increasing motivation

Clarifying value

Offering more support

  1. Distractions: How can we keep suppliers focused? Might we reevaluate what we show suppliers given their stage in the process, such as call outs to paid features?

  2. User motivation: How might we customize the onboarding flow to the supplier? Can we take information they share with us and use it as motivation to get them through the product form?

  3. RangeMe's value prop: Can we show RangeMe's value to suppliers in a more compelling and memorable way?

  4. In-product support: For areas that suppliers frequently have problems with, how can we integrate scalable ways to help them?

Reducing distractions

Focusing on suppliers who reached Home, we evaluated all the elements that suppliers saw and realized that we give prominence to less crucial information and steps. To help guide suppliers complete their profile, we introduced changes that reduced the amount of steps and time needed to reach the product form.

Shortening our welcome modal

Previously our welcome modal showed 3 slides. Two of the three slides were irrelevant given their stage of the funnel (the 1st slide gave a basic summary about RangeMe, the 3rd described a paid feature). After getting through the slides, suppliers were taken to Home and presented with a checklist of tasks to complete their profile.

To help suppliers stay on track, we condensed the welcome modal to one screen that explains suppliers why they should add their product and takes them straight to adding their first product.

Changing the 1st step in our onboarding checklist to "Add product"

After completing the first two pages of sign up, suppliers are brought to their Home, where they see a checklist that helps them complete their profile. Previously, the first task they saw was "Confirm email." Out of all the tasks, "Add product" is most valuable for suppliers. By making it the 1st step to complete, it becomes more visible and provides additional context about why they should complete it.

Results so far

When my team and I launched the changes in 2021, we reduced distractions in our welcome modal and checklist and shortened the amount of time to get suppliers to the product form. Compared to earlier this year, we have seen:

+4.51%

more suppliers entering the product form

+1.24%

number of suppliers who add their first product

While this is only 1 of 4 areas we will be making improvements to, we're excited to see the uptick in the number of suppliers who have completed or become closer to completing their profile. This overall project is still in progress and the next set of changes will be focused on supplier personalization and motivation. Please check back as more updates are launched!
