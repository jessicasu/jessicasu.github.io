Notification Center

During the latter half of 2020, I designed RangeMe's first notification center that was launched to over 7,000+ buyers and 150,000+ suppliers.

Background

RangeMe is an B2B online marketplace that connects suppliers (brands like Coca Cola, Oreos, Chico Honey Co.) and buyers (retailers such as Target, Whole Foods Market, Safeway). The RangeMe platform lets supplier create online company profiles to showcase their products, which buyers can discover and reach out to.

The Problem

As RangeMe becomes a more interactive platform, there is an increasing need to provide timely, valuable notifications to our suppliers and buyers. We currently rely on emails and banners to communicate with our suppliers and buyers. However, we face several issues:

Our emails have a low click through rate.

We often rely on banners to notify users in the platform, resulting in a stacked banner situation.

Rethinking Our Communication Channels

In the platform, we used banners to communicate urgent, high priority updates. Everything else fell under emails. Banners are effective at presenting users with high-visibility updates; however, as we used them more often, they started to lose their prominence as they started to stack on top of each other.

We decided an update would be sent through a particular communication channel depending on its level of importance. In product, banners would be used for extremely important updates in product that is crucial to setting up users' profile while updates that are nice to know but not essential would live in the notification center. Outside of the platform, emails would be used for both critical and non-essential updates to engage with users outside the platform and help get them back into RangeMe.

Emails

Valuable updates that bring the user back to the platform

Banners

Updates that need high visible and crucial to their account

Notification

Updates that are nice to know and are contained in the platform

Goals

To handle the increasing number of notifications and decide where they should live, we introduced another in-product communication channel and aligned on how we would use emails and banners. The notification center would:

  • Offer a more extensive and scalable way to communicate with our users

  • Reduce the number of banners and emails

  • Increase the discoverability of timely notifications

RangeMe's Notification Center

The notification window

Users can find their notification center as part of their navigation bar. The minimized window view offers a less disruptive way for users to view without taking them out of the page they were previously looking at. The notifications are divided up into a two groupings denoted by color and a "Manage" notification link to let especially our slightly tech-savy audience customize what they see.

Relevant notifications

Notification fatigue is common in everyday apps and I didn't want RangeMe to face the same pitfall. We created each of our notifications to be informative, relevant, and actionable. We launched this MVP with 5 supplier notifications and 4 buyer notifications that would help the user with their discovery or sourcing needs.

Results

In September 2020, my teammates and I launched the notification center, an alternative communication channel to banners and emails. As part of its introduction:

2

stacking banner scenarios have been prevented on the supplier side

9

new notifications have been created for buyers and suppliers

36k+

users have interacted with the notification center within a month

We're excited to see suppliers and buyers opening their notifications and completing key actions. The notification center has given us a scalable way to share timely updates with our users in the platform and a place to house future notifications.
