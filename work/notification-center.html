Notification Center

During the latter half of 2020, I designed RangeMe's first ever notification center for our suppliers and buyers.

Role: UX Designer

Teammates: Will (PM), Jared (PMM), Adrien (Eng Lead)

Status: Launched

Background

What is RangeMe?

RangeMe is an online marketplace that connects suppliers (brands like Coca Cola, Oreos, Chico Honey Co.) and buyers (retailers such as Target, Whole Foods Market, Safeway). The RangeMe platform lets supplier create online company profiles to showcase their products, which buyers can discover and reach out to.

The Problem

As RangeMe becomes a more interactive platform, there is an increasing need to provide timely, valuable notifications to our suppliers and buyers. We currently rely on emails and banners to communicate with our suppliers and buyers. However, we face several issues:

Our emails have a low click through rate

We often rely on banners to notify users in the platform, resulting in a stacked banner situation

Rethinking Our Communication Channels

In the platform, we used banners to communicate urgent, high priority updates. Everything else fell under emails. Banners are effective at presenting users with high-visibility updates; however, as we used them more often, they started to lose their prominence as they started to stack on top of each other.

To handle the increasing number of notifications and decide where they should live, we introduced another in-product communication channel — the notification center — and aligned on the purpose of each channel that we offer.

We decided an update would be sent through a particular communication channel depending on its level of importance. In product, banners would be used for extremely important updates in product that is crucial to setting up users' profile ("Your account is missing important information...") while updates that are nice to know but not essential to using RangeMe would live in the notification center ("Your teammate hasn't invited your invite"). Outside of the platform, emails would be used for both critical and non-essential updates to engage with users outside the platform and help get them back into RangeMe.

Goals

Our team planned to introduce a notification center to:

  • Offer a more extensive and scalable way to communicate with our users

  • Reduce the number of banners and emails

  • Increase the discoverability of timely notifications

RangeMe's Notification Center

We launched the notification center to both suppliers and buyers in September 2020. With its introduction, we have prevented stacking banner situations and now offer an alternative way to communicate with our users in the platform.

The notification window

Users can find their notification center as part of their navigation bar. The minimized window view offers a less disruptive way for users to view without taking them out of the page they were previously looking at. The notifications are divided up into a two groupings denoted by color and a "Manage" notification link to let especially our slightly tech-savy audience easily customize what they see.

Useful notifications

Notification fatigue is common in everyday apps and I didn't want RangeMe to face the same pitfall. Each of our notifications is informative, relevant, and actionable. We launched this MVP with 8 supplier notifications and 3 buyer notifications that would help the user with their discovery or sourcing needs.
